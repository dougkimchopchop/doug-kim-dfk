The Cliff

Escape hatch removal — not catastrophe, irreversibility

The actual diagnosis
You don't have a courage problem. You've proven you'll do hard things.
You have an escape hatch problem. The apartment is always there, always comfortable, always winning.
A cliff doesn't motivate you. It simplifies the decision down to zero. That's the actual mechanism.
Ambiguity wins every morning by default. Not YouTube. Ambiguity.
Mechanism vs. mythology
What's real
Binary switches work for you. Removing the option to retreat works for you. Consequence works for you.
What's a story
That commitment has to mean destroying your safety net or manufacturing genuine panic. Real desperation narrows your thinking — it makes the work worse. Don't confuse becoming desperate with becoming committed.
The constitution — not goals, laws
  • I do not spend daylight hours in the apartment unless I'm sick, working my day job, or it's an explicitly scheduled exception. Sleep, shower, cook, dog care — that's what the apartment is for. Nothing else.
  • I publish every Thursday. Bad essay — publish. Nothing to say — publish. No version of Thursday where nothing goes live.
  • I make one photograph a day I'd sign my name to. Not 200 random frames. One. Every day. No zero days.
  • No laptop, no internet, no lounging at home. Writing, editing, pitching happens outside the apartment.
  • I do not consume before I've created. No scrolling, no YouTube, until today's output already exists.
  • I make myself visible. Send the pitch. Post the unfinished work. Show up. Hiding is the comfort you're addicted to, not the couch.
Today's exception — logged, not excused
Not a new rule
Work deadline and post-guest cleanup. Today the apartment is a workday base, not a hotel. That's a fact about today. Tomorrow the laws resume with zero grace period. Log tonight whether that's true.
Why this isn't the same as being harder on yourself
Shame tells you who you are. A law tells you what you do next. You don't need to feel like DFK to follow law 03 today — you just follow it. The feeling is downstream, not a prerequisite. Stop auditing your motivation and start checking the log.
The cliff was never the danger. It was deleting the thousand small decisions that let you choose comfort. Delete them again — on purpose — and the person who watches YouTube at 9am doesn't get a vote anymore.
