Intention

Choose the person. Choose the feeling. Make them feel it. The mantra, the punch list, and the ladder — in order.

The Mantra
Full version
"Choose the person. Choose the feeling. Make them feel it."
Say before sitting down to write.
Quick-trigger version
"One face. One feeling. Go."
Fire this the moment a draft stalls mid-sentence.
The Punch List
01
Before You Sit Down
  1. Say the mantra: "Choose the person. Choose the feeling. Make them feel it."
  2. Pick one real, specific person to write toward — not "an audience"
02
Find the Material
  1. Dump raw fragments first — no grammar, no shaping
  2. Hunt the dump for an accidental pattern or echo
  3. Lower the bar — one true, weird, concrete detail is enough
  4. No pattern surfacing? Force an arbitrary container (top ten, countdown, Q&A)
03
Aim It
  1. Fire the quick-trigger the moment a draft stalls: "One face. One feeling. Go."
  2. Name the one person and one feeling out loud before the next sentence
04
Build It, In Order
  1. Write sentence by sentence — don't reach for the paragraph yet
  2. Finish the paragraph before advancing — done means it holds weight, not that it's long enough
  3. Build out the scene or throughline the paragraph belongs to
  4. Move to the next unit — repeat the ladder, don't jump around polishing out of order
05
Make It Reader-Facing
  1. Address one named, real person for the piece (Baldwin move) — forces stakes
  2. Or address the reader as "you," making them complicit (Dawes / Bambara move)
  3. Write toward an absent recipient if the material is a letter that was never sent
06
Mid-Draft Checks
  1. Flat paragraph? Check mood before tone — mood is usually what's missing
  2. Spend your best image now — don't save it for later
  3. If you're not discovering something, you're executing, not writing — go back to the dump
The full arc in one line: dump → hunt → mantra → sentence → paragraph → scene → next.
Excavation before construction. Discovery before architecture.
