Identity · Execution · No Exits

Action and hard work is a behavior you do right now.

01 — the only definition that matters
Hard work is starting before you're ready.
Grit is not inspiration. It's sitting down and doing the next task when every part of you wants to think about doing it instead. You are excellent at thinking about doing it. That is the problem. The hard worker starts. The planner waits for the right moment. The right moment never comes.
02 — discipline is not a personality trait
Discipline is the gap between "I don't feel like it" and doing it anyway.
You don't need to feel disciplined. You need to act before the feeling has a vote. The people you think are disciplined aren't motivated. They've just eliminated the negotiation. No one asks themselves "do I feel like brushing my teeth?" You do the habit. The feeling is irrelevant.
03 — what grit actually is
Grit is choosing discomfort over disappointment.
Every day you don't ship, you trade 5 minutes of discomfort (pressing send, posting the photo, hitting publish) for 24 hours of low-grade self-loathing. You are already paying the price. You're just paying it for nothing.
04 — how to become it
You become a hard worker by working hard. Today. Not by understanding it.
This question — "what is a hard worker, how do I become one" — is the avoidance. You are doing it right now. The search for the answer is the retreat from the answer. The answer is to close this and open a document and write one sentence.
The lies you're telling yourself right now
  • I need to understand discipline better before I can practice it.
  • I'll start once I feel motivated.
  • Reading about grit is building grit.
  • Today is special. I'll get it together tomorrow.
  • This question deserves a thoughtful answer before I act.
