Anti-Avoidance Protocol

THE SYSTEM

Identity statement — write this somewhere visible
"I am someone who does the hard thing first."
01 — One Thing
Every night — write it down
The task you'd most avoid tomorrow. Not a list. One thing. Everything else is secondary.
02 — The Real Work Test
Ask before every task
"Did I produce something or move something forward that did not exist or was not done before I sat down?"
YES — Real work. Keep going.
  • Words written
  • Photo submitted
  • Person contacted
  • Decision made
  • Thing cleaned / fixed / done
NO — Avoidance. Stop. Do the one thing.
  • Planning to do the thing
  • Researching how to do the thing
  • Organizing tools to do the thing
  • Thinking about doing the thing
  • Reading about people who do the thing
Output only counts. Process is invisible.
02b — The Quality Checkpoint
Before you cross anything off
"Is this the best I am capable of — or did I just get it done to get it done?"
Sit with that question. Answer it honestly. If you phoned it in: do not cross it off. Do not move on. Find the one thing that would make it genuinely good. Do that thing.

You know what your best work feels like. You've done it before. Anything without that feeling is not done yet.

Half-assing is avoidance in disguise. Crossing off a phoned-in task is lying to yourself. It counts for nothing.
03 — The Avoidance Interrupt
When you feel the drift
Say or write: "I am avoiding ___ right now."
Then do 5 minutes of the real thing. Just 5.
04 — One Frozen Area Per Week
Pick one. One small action. Specific day + time. Tell someone.
Not "be more social" → "Text one person Tuesday at 7pm."
05 — Nightly 3-Question Review
3 minutes. Every night. Non-negotiable.
1. What did I actually do today?
2. What did I avoid — and why?
3. What is the one thing tomorrow?
06 — The Identity Statement
Write it somewhere visible
"I am someone who does the hard thing first."
Every action is a vote for or against this. Win the vote over time.
The possibility that you could have been great, if only you'd tried —
that possibility is worth nothing. It is a lie you are paying for with your actual life.
Two minutes. Not the essay. Not the pitch. Not the shoot.
Two minutes of the thing.
Do not modify. Do not improve. Do not research better versions.

START TONIGHT.
